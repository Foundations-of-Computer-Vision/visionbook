Pinhole Camera Controls

Perspective Projection

A pinhole camera projects 3D points P = (X, Y, Z) to 2D positions p = (x, y) on the projection plane via:

x = f · X / Z     y = f · Y / Z

Projected: x = —, y = —

The Z-axis is the optical axis. The projection plane sits at distance f behind the pinhole. The virtual camera plane mirrors it in front, avoiding image inversion.
