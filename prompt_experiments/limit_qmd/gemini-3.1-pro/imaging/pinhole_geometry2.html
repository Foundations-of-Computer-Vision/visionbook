Concept: Pinhole Geometry

This interactive scene demonstrates the geometry of a pinhole camera.

  • Pinhole: Located at the origin (0,0,0).
  • Point P: A 3D point in the world $(X, Y, Z)$.
  • Projection Plane: Located behind the pinhole at $Z = -f$. The image is inverted.
  • Virtual Camera Plane: Located in front of the pinhole at $Z = f$. The image is upright.

Notice how adjusting the focal length $f$ changes the size of the projected point $\mathbf{p}$ on both planes.
